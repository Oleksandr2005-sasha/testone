Solar Energy

Solar energy harnesses the power of the sun to generate electricity or heat. It is a clean and renewable energy source.

There are two main types of solar energy technologies: photovoltaic (PV) systems, which convert sunlight directly into electricity, and solar thermal systems, which use sunlight to heat water or other fluids.

Wind Energy

Wind energy uses turbines to convert the kinetic energy of wind into electrical power. It is one of the fastest-growing energy technologies.

Wind farms can be located onshore or offshore, with offshore wind farms typically having higher wind speeds and more consistent wind patterns.

Hydropower

Hydropower, or water power, uses the energy of flowing water to produce electricity. It is a reliable and sustainable energy source.

There are several types of hydropower plants, including run-of-the-river, storage, and pumped storage plants, each with different methods of harnessing water energy.

Geothermal Energy

Geothermal energy utilizes the heat from the Earth's interior to generate electricity and provide heating. It is a stable and sustainable energy source.

Geothermal power plants can be built in areas with high geothermal activity, such as near tectonic plate boundaries or hot spots.

Biomass Energy

Biomass energy is produced from organic materials such as plant and animal waste. It can be used for heating, electricity generation, and as biofuel.

Common sources of biomass include wood, agricultural residues, and dedicated energy crops. Biomass can be converted into energy through processes such as combustion, gasification, and anaerobic digestion.
